Our methodology

We show our working.
Every step of it.

Every valuation is built from official data — HM Land Registry sold prices, the ONS House Price Index, EPC floor areas — run through ten valuation methods and weighted by data quality. Then we score how much the result can be trusted, and print that score on the report.

🏛️

HM Land Registry

Every residential property sold in England and Wales since 1995. Real transaction prices — not estimates.

📈

ONS House Price Index

Official UK government regional price index, used to adjust every past sold price to today's market before we compare anything.

📐

EPC Register

Energy Performance Certificate data gives us the property's floor area — powering the price-per-square-metre analysis.

📋

Listing & market data

Days on market, price reductions and local asking-to-sold gaps — the public signals of how much room a seller has to move.

⚖️

Ten valuation methods, weighted

No single method is trusted on its own. Each one values the property independently, and the final range weights them by data quality.

●

Confidence-scored — always

Every report says HIGH, MEDIUM or LOW confidence and why. When the data is thin we widen the range and tell you — we never hide uncertainty.

FAQ

Questions we get asked
all the time

Is this the same as a RICS survey? ↓
No — HouseOffer is an independent data analysis tool to help you decide what to offer, not a structural survey. We always recommend a full survey before exchanging contracts.
Will the estate agent know I've used HouseOffer? ↓
No. Your report is private — we never share information with estate agents, developers, or any third party. Our model is built on being entirely independent from the selling side.
Why should I trust your number over the estate agent's? ↓
The estate agent is paid by the seller, and the valuation that wins the listing is usually the flattering one. Our number has no side to please: it's built from what similar homes actually sold for (HM Land Registry), adjusted to today's market with the ONS House Price Index, and cross-checked across ten independent valuation methods. And unlike anyone else, we tell you how much to trust the result — every report carries a HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW confidence score with the reason in plain English.
What does HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW confidence mean? ↓
It's our honest score of how strong the data behind your valuation is. HIGH means we found genuinely comparable sales — matched on size or bedrooms — that agree with each other. MEDIUM means the evidence is decent but less directly matched. LOW means the data is thin or the property is unusual, and you should treat the number with caution. In our latest validation run, HIGH-confidence reports landed within 10% of the asking price 56% of the time — and the score is doing its job precisely because HIGH beats MEDIUM beats LOW.
What if my report comes back LOW confidence? ↓
We tell you, we widen the ranges, and we never hide the uncertainty. LOW confidence usually means something real: thin local sold data, an unusual property, or a listing that isn't standard market stock (auctions, shared ownership, retirement schemes — we detect and flag these). Most valuation sites would hand you a confident-looking number anyway. We think knowing the number is shaky is worth more than pretending it isn't.
Some sites say homes sell 17% below asking — why is your number smaller? ↓
Because those figures compare the average asking price of homes currently listed with the average price of homes that sold — two different sets of homes, so the gap comes out inflated (unsold, overpriced stock drags the listed average up). We compare each sold home with its own asking price, which is the question you actually care about: how much below asking do homes like this one really go for?
Is this financial or professional advice? ↓
No. HouseOffer reports are information only. They are not a RICS valuation, not a survey, and not financial advice. We show you the data and how we read it — the decision, and the offer, are always yours. For a mortgage or structural assessment, use a qualified professional.
